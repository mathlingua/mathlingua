Limits

Now we have the tools needed to define a fundamental topic of real analysis, the notion of a limit.

Specifically, the notion of a limit formalizes the idea that in real analysis, if one cannot evaluate $f(c)$ for some real number $c$ and some real-valued function $f$, then one can still get information about $f(c)$ by analyzing $f(x)$ for values of $x$ that are arbitrarily close to $c$.

Limits will be essential for describing the derivative of a function.
